How NOT to die in dumb ways
Don't Use a Toaster in the Bathtub

Water and electricity don't mix. Keep your toaster in the kitchen, not your bathroom.

Avoid Eating Random Berries in the Forest

Just because they look tasty doesn't mean they won't make you sick or worse. Stick to berries you know are safe.

Don't Use a Blow Dryer in the Shower

Your hair can wait. Using electrical appliances in the shower is a shockingly bad idea.

Don't Stand on a Rolling Chair to Reach High Places

Use a ladder or a stable stool instead. Rolling chairs are for sitting, not acrobatics.

Don't Stick Metal Objects in Electrical Sockets

Curiosity killed the cat, and it can also cause a nasty shock. Keep forks, knives, and other metal objects out of electrical outlets.

Avoid Playing with Wild Animals

  • That bear might look cuddly, but it's not a teddy bear. Admire wildlife from a safe distance.

    • Don't Try to Take a Selfie with a Charging Bull

  • Some photos aren't worth the risk. Bulls are not fans of selfies.

    • Don't Use a Chainsaw as a Haircut Tool

  • Hairdressers have scissors for a reason. Chainsaws are for trees, not hair.

    • Avoid Mixing Unknown Chemicals for Fun

  • Leave the chemistry experiments to professionals. Mixing random household cleaners can produce toxic fumes.

    • Don't Test if Your Superhero Cape Really Works

  • Capes look cool, but they don't grant you the power of flight. Stick to ground-level activities.
